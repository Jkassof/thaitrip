Wed, 12/12/18

Old Bangkok Walking Tour

Start the day with an early power breakfast in the hotel. From there we will take a hotel provided boat up the river to Chiang Pier where we will begin a 4km, 4-6 hour tour of old bangkok. We will hit the world famous Bangkok templates like Wat Pho (Temple of the Reclining Buddha) and Wat Phra Kaew (Temple of Jade Buddha) & the Grand Palace, as well as a number of other lesser known spots.

The bulk of Bangkok's must-see desitnations are in and around the former royal district Ko Ratanakosin. It's best to start early to beat both the heat and the hordes. Remember to dress modestly to gain entry to the temples. Also ignore any strangers offering advice on sightseeing or shopping.

Start at 1) Chiang Pier. Proceed east to the main gate of 2) Wat Phra Kaew & Grand Palace (p61), two of Bangkok's most famous attractions. Backtrack, then head north along Th Maha Rat, and immediately afer passing the cat-laden newsstand (you'll know it when you smell it), turn left into 3) Trok Tha Wang, a narrow allewway holding a hidden classic Bangkok neighborhood.

Turn left into crowded Trok Maha That to discover the cramped 4) amulet market (p68). Exiting the market at Tha Phra Chan, cross Th Maha Rat and continue east until you reach 5) Sanam Luang, the "Royal Field."

Cross Th Ratchadamnoen Nai and go north, turning right at the Royal Hotel onto 6) Th Ratchadamnoen Klang, Bangkok's own Champs-Elysees Continuing east, at the intersection with Th Tanae, you'll see the 7) October 14 memorial, commemorating the civiliation demonstrators who were killed on 14 October 1973 by the military during a pro-democracy rally. In the distance you'll see the 8) Democracy Monument, erected in 1932.

After a lunch stop at 9) Pok Spa Kar or 10) Chote Chitr, enter Th Phraeng Nara, crossing Khlong Lod, and continue west along Th Lak Meuang to the home of Bankok's city spirit, 11) Lak Meuang (p69).

Head south along the Sanam Chai and turn right onto Th Thai Wang, which will escourt you to the entrance of 12) Wat Pho (p61), home of the giant Reclining Buddha.

After that long afternoon of navigating the streets of old Bangkok, we will likely be pretty sweaty and gross. Time to head back to the hotel to clean up and get ready for a night out in Bangkok.
